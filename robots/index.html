The Toucan



Winner of the 2014 VEX Robotics World Championship Innovate Award

Created to compete in VEX Toss Up

The Toucan was designed for fast offensive scoring. Its high lift capabilities and roller intake allowed The Toucan to quickly score three Buckyballs at once. Its shifting X drive gave it high speed and maneuverability while allowing it to easily shift to tank drive for pushing power. The Toucan could also pick up large balls with a pneumatic claw.

Robot Profile

Purpose:
  Fast, offensive scoring
Drive:
  - Shifting X
  - 4 high strength motors
  - Shifts to any ratio between 1:1 and 1:1.5
Lift:
  34" Scissors
Intake:
  Top roller for Buckyballs + pneumatic claw for large balls
Features:
  - Fast, reliable pickup of both types of balls anywhere on the field
  - Can stash both large balls and Buckyballs
  - Multiple autonomous modes for both zones
  - Pneumatic intake release for fast outtake

OK

Created to compete in BEST Gatekeeper

The R-CHIMP (Robotics Contributing to Hi-tech Innovative Manufacturing and Placement) was designed for rapidly producing CPUs. It featured two separate arms, one to handle transistor containers and logic gates, and the other to place CPU pieces. The first consisted of a series of claws to pick up multiple objects at once. The other featured a single extending claw for precise long distance manipulation. Both arms could slide along tracks perpendicular to the direction of the trolley’s movement to increase range of motion. To further increase speed and precision, sensors were used to stop the slide at the ends of its motion, and to adjust arm control parameters.

OK

Created to compete in BEST Warp XX

E.T. was a robotic space elevator. Its base consisted of two polypropylene plates connected by PVC columns. This provided an exceptionally sturdy platform for the rest of the robot. A winch was connected to the bottom platform of E.T. to enable it to move up and down the tower. A plywood sleeve ensured a smooth and stable climb. An arm was connected to the base via a turntable. Consisting of two aluminum and PVC "ladders", the arm was light and sturdy. It had two joints. This setup allowed for maximum maneuverability. The pulley system used to move the arm amplified power and kept the heavy motors off of the arm. E.T.'s claw manipulator could twist, bend, and grab for unmatched versatility. E.T.'s cargo basket attachment meant that the robot only needed to make one trip into space, increasing efficiency.

Created to compete in VRC Gateway

The Roadrunner was designed for quickly gathering and scoring game elements. An expanding intake tray gave the Roadrunner a 7 object capacity. A six bar lift allowed the Roadrunner to score in all heights of goals, and reach into the opponent's isolation 20" goal. A servo powered finger assisted when receiving match loads or when holding 5+ objects. Sensors on the robot included potentiometers for arm presets, line sensors for autonomous alignment, shaft encoders on the drive wheels, and limit switches to select the autonomous mode.

OK

Created to compete in VRC Round Up

Version 1 of the Duck used a servo powered claw to lift and score stacks of objects. It could score on both heights of posts.

Versions 2 and 3 of the Duck utilized a polycarbonate forklift to pick up and score up to four rings at a time. The forklift could descore any number of scored objects. Version 2 featured rotating tabs at the front and back of the drive that allowed the Duck to "eat" rings. Version 3 replaced this feature with a mechanism to tilt the lift to aid when scoring the high posts and when descoring.
